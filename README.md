# Zhouli-Data-Analysis
The Zhouli, or Rights of the Zhou, is a 2nd BC description of court organization and bureacracy in ancient China.  It contains something like a spreadsheet of staff, jobs, departments, which I have analyzed.
I used rudimentary Japanese to identify simple Chinese characters indicating numbers (1-9) and person.  AI, with context, was able to translate some of the Chinese, specifically the "spreadsheet" sections.
Numbers of people are easier to translate than the actual descriptions of duties, ceremonies, etc.  

Conclusions come from analyzing a .csv created from the Zhouli.txt using google translate combined with chatgpt's translate as well as annotations on the text and historical context.  The resulting numbers
paint an image of a Zhou capital palace.  Over 6000 personel served in 376 roles.  The organization that the Zhouli describes was hierarchical, with large numbers of servants being overseen by  
management that scales with the total number of workers.  

Jobs vary widely and are divided by the books they appear in.

Offices of the Heaven (天官冢宰; Tiānguān Zhǒngzǎi) on general principles of governance, appointment of officials, guards, attendants and royal purveyors of provisions such as wine, jade, dried meat, vinegar... Descriptions on duties and responsibilities of officials, eunuchs and concubines;
Offices of Earth (地官司徒; Dìguān Sītú) on local governance, agriculture, taxation and division of land;
Offices of Spring (春官宗伯; Chūnguān Zōngbó) on ceremonies, music, rituals and divinatory practices;
Offices of Summer (夏官司馬; Xiàguān Sīmǎ) on the organization of the army;
Office of Autumn (秋官司寇; Qiūguān Sīkòu) on justice, punishments, legal system and court rites;
Office of Winter (冬官考工記; Dōngguān Kǎogōngjì) on public works, manufacturing, artisans, craftmanship and art.

The Zhouli, with data analysis, gives us a very thorough and detailed description of a working capital and all the different positions within its bureacratic structure.
